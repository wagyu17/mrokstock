## The relationship between **Page Quality** and **Needs Met**
The **Needs Met rating** is related to both **query and the result.** The Page Quality rating does not depend on the query.
- Useless results should always be rated FailsM, even if the landing page has a high Page Quality rating.
- The **HM rating is not appropriate** if a **page is untrustworthy** or has any other undesirable characteristics, such as outdated or inaccurate information. There are very high standards for the HM rating.
- A page can have such low Page Quality that it is unhelpful or even harmful. These **low quality pages should be rated FailsM** because the PQ issues interfere with helpfulness, *except for queries with clear website intent*, where the target website should be rated FullyM.
- **Remember**: **Fully Meets is a special category** that is rarely used and for a result to get a Fully Meets rating, **the query & user need must be specific, clear, and unambiguous.**
- *Some Special Content Result Blocks* may have links to landing pages. In these cases, **think about whether a user would click on the link** in order to satisfy their user need. If some or many users would click, both the content of the block and the landing page need to be helpful.

## **Query** Interpretation
Interpreting the query is a crucial part of rating Needs Met. By “query” we mean what the user types or speaks into a mobile phone.
Queries can generally be divided into 4 categories based on intent, a query can fit into one or more of these categories:
- **Know query:** The intent is to find information on a topic. Most queries look for broad, complex and in-depth information without a short answer. Other queries look for a very specific answer, known as **Know Simple** queries. Know Simple queries can have a FullyM result while **broader Know queries** cannot. *e.g. “How many Grams in Kilograms?” (Know Simple) vs. “What is the meaning of life?” (Broad)*
- **Do query:** The intent is to accomplish a goal or engage in an activity on a phone. Device Action queries are a special kind of Do query. Users are asking their phone to do something for them, using a clear action word and intent. *e.g. “BMI calculator”, “The Avengers Trailer” and “Spaghetti Bolognese Recipe”*
- **Website query:** The intent is to locate a specific website or webpage that users have requested. *e.g. “facebook login”, “amazon.com” and “Toy Story imdb”*
- **Visit-in-person query:** The intent of a VIP query is to find a physical location. Please **pay particular attention to User Location** for these queries as this can have a big impact on the Needs Met rating. *e.g. “Gas stations near me” and “Movie Showtimes”*
*Some queries can have multiple intents*. e.g. the query, “Disneyland” can have Visit-In-Person, Website and/or Know intent.
While some queries with **Dominant Interpretation can have FullyM result**, *queries with multiple common interpretations cannot receive a FullyM rating.*
