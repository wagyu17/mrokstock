このガイドラインは、Apple Podcastsの検索アルゴリズムとランキングを向上させるためのレーティング基準を提供している.以下のように重要なポイントを要約する:
**Apple Podcastsとは**
Apple Podcastsは、あらゆるジャンルの人気ポッドキャストを提供するプラットフォームで、個人制作から大規模プロジェクトまで幅広くカバーしている.
**レーティング作業の目的**
高品質な評価データは、検索アルゴリズムやランキングの向上に利用され、ユーザー体験を向上させる.評価者は以下を重視する必要がある:
•	クエリと結果の関連性
> 💡 クエリと結果の関連性とは、検索クエリ（ユーザーが入力した検索語句やフレーズ）が、検索結果（表示されたページやコンテンツ）とどれだけ一致し、ユーザーの意図を満たしているかを評価する概念のことだ.
  **主な要素**
  1.	**検索意図**
  •	クエリからユーザーが何を求めているのかを理解すること.
  •	例: クエリ「Lil Baby」は、特定の人物（アメリカのラッパー）や関連する音楽・コンテンツを求めていると推測できる.
  2.	**一致度**
  •	結果の内容が、クエリで期待されるものとどれだけ一致しているか.
  •	タイトル、説明、提供元が直接関連しているほど一致度が高い.
  3.	**品質と信頼性**
  •	結果がユーザーにとって役立つ情報を提供しているか.
  •	信頼性のある提供元や高品質なコンテンツであることも重要.
  4.	**人気度・話題性**
  •	人気のあるコンテンツや、多くの人が関心を持つ情報は、より関連性が高いとみなされる場合がある.
•	人気度や品質
•	クエリ意図の理解（プライマリ意図とセカンダリ意図）
**評価基準**
•	**Perfect**: クエリで求められた特定のポッドキャストやエピソードそのもの.
•	**Excellent**: クエリのプライマリ意図を完全に満たす高品質なコンテンツ.
•	**Good**: 一部満たすが、やや低品質またはセカンダリ意図を満たすもの.
•	**Acceptable**: 関連性はあるが低品質またはニッチな対象向け.
•	**Unacceptable**: クエリと無関係、または誤解を招くコンテンツ.
**注意点**
•	**人気度の評価**: 他のプラットフォーム（Spotify、YouTubeなど）も活用して人気度を評価する.
•	**タイプの違い**: ポッドキャストはエピソード型（最新順）とシリーズ型（シーズン単位）に分かれる.
•	**多言語クエリ**: 翻訳ツールを活用し、各言語や市場における関連性を評価する.
•	**誤字の対応**: 意図された正しいクエリを想定して評価する.
**適用シナリオ**
1.	**ナビゲーションクエリ**: 特定のポッドキャスト、エピソード、またはチャンネルを指す.
2.	**カテゴリクエリ**: [ニュース]、[コメディ]などのジャンル全般を検索する.
3.	**トピック/キーワードクエリ**: 特定の話題や人物を検索する.
**総括**
評価者は、検索クエリの意図を正確に理解し、関連性、品質、人気度を基に適切なレーティングを行うことが求められる.

## **Helpful tips & tricks in rating the results**

### How to assess the popularity of a Podcasts content
To define how popular content is, you must research different platforms and **not** rely only on Apple Podcasts' user signals. For example, Google Podcasts, Google Search, and Spotify are great resources, but you will not have the user signals ( number of reviews and star avg) available; hence, you would need to look at how high on the Search Results Page the intended content is being ranked. Usually, content closer to the top of the page is popular on those platforms.Many Podcasts are also video casts, and creators most of the time have their content on Youtube, making this platform very useful for measuring a Podcast Show's ( number of subscribers in the YouTube channel) and an episode's ( number of views) popularity.Make sure to use reliable Podcasts platforms from your country to assess popularity - please provide the link to it.**Remember:** use the Apple Podcasts app as a starting point rather than a definitive popularity signal resource.

### **Apple Podcasts Top Charts**
[Apple Podcasts Charts](https://podcasters.apple.com/support/3146-apple-podcasts-charts) present a dynamic view of the most popular and trending content on Apple Podcasts. It is a good way to identify popular content across various markets within Apple Podcasts.

### Find the charts
On iOS and iPadOS, listeners can easily find Top Charts on the [Search](https://podcasts.apple.com/search) tab. On macOS and tvOS, they’re located under Charts. Listeners can also find Top Shows, Top Episodes, Top Subscriber Shows, and Top Subscriber Channels featured throughout the Browse tab on iOS, iPadOS, and macOS, or by navigating to the [Charts](https://podcasts.apple.com/browse/top-charts) page. ### What the charts measure
Apple Podcasts Charts reflect the most popular shows and episodes available in a given market and are designed to help people discover what to listen to next. They do not reflect all-time listening records and are not a measure of the largest podcasts by listenership.

### Researching Query Intent
We expect you to use search engines such as Bing, Google, etc., to help you understand the intent of each query. For example, searching "[query] podcast" or "[query] apple podcasts" will help narrow down the intent.

### Primary and Secondary intent:
The **primary intent **of a query is the most likely intent, i.e., the intent of most users who enter that query in Apple Podcasts.On the other hand, a** secondary intent** is less likely or would be a less popular intent than a primary one. A secondary intent could be:
- content relevant to a smaller group of users than for the primary intent
- complimentary content; such as reviews or summaries of the intended show
- lower quality/lesser-known content

### Spell Correction
Our users misspell some queries. When this is the case, assume the query intent is for the correctly spelled query.If the query is **[mark kilmek]**, the user meant to type **[mark klimek]**. Use your best judgment.

### Adult Content
We assume that all adult or pornographic content is Off-Topic for non-adult and non-porn queries. For example, any adult or porn content is Unacceptable: Off-Topic for the query [popular podcasts] because the user has not expressly indicated that they want the content of this type. On the other hand, if the query is [erotica] or [porn], any adult or porn content is relevant.

### Rating Non-English Queries
Please note that Apple Podcasts users query for content in multiple languages.All queries in all languages are valid search queries, and the results should be rated based on relevance and quality, just like with any other type of query. Please utilize translation tools and conduct thorough side searches to understand the likely user intent.For example, suppose the user enters the Spanish language query [liderazgo hoy]. In that case, they're looking for the "[Liderazgo Hoy con Victor Hugo Manzanilla](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/liderazgo-hoy-con-victor-hugo-manzanilla/id617594510)" Podcast, which would be rated Perfect. Results should be in either the language of the query, market language, or in English (OK for all markets).**Note:** If the result is in a different language than the query, with no apparent relevance to that market or direct title match then the rating should be Unacceptable: Off-Topic. For example, user enters the English language query [politics] and the result is German channel [RND RedaktionsNetzwerk], the rating would be Unacceptable: Off-TopicFor example, in the US market a result in Spanish would be relevant but considered secondary intent.

## Rating Scale for Queries with a singular, navigational result
- **Perfect**: The singular piece or collection of content implied by the query. Reserved for the **Navigational** result.
- **Excellent**: A piece of content that clearly satisfies a likely **primary** intent of the query.
- **Good**: A piece of content that only partially satisfies the intent of the query but is still high-quality**or** satisfies the intent of the query but is lower quality**or **satisfies a secondary intent of the query.
- **Acceptable**: A piece of content that is related to the query but may be of poor quality, not popular, or poorly satisfies a secondary intent of the query.
- **Unacceptable: Off-Topic**: A piece of content that does not satisfy any of the above conditions.
- **Unacceptable: Misleading **This rating should be used for content that is likely to mislead users about its authorship intentionally and/or non-podcast content.
- **Problem: Other**: A problem with the task in BaseLine; for example, the result image thumbnail and the result show/episode title are missing, making it impossible to rate the result.

## How to rate Episodes results when the query type is Podcast Show Navigational

### Episodic type of Podcasts
**Rating:Excellent:**
- All the recent or latest* episodes,** except trailer episodes,** from the intended show (Episodes in both video and audio types)
- Sometimes the intended Podcast Show stop releasing new episodes. The **latest released episodes** are the most recent ones and should be rated as Excellent.
**Good:**
- Trailer episodes of the intended show
- Episodes results from the intended podcast show would receive a Good rating if it is older than the below thresholds:
  - Daily release frequency: episode **older than** 1-2 weeks
  - Semi-weekly frequency: episode **older than** 3 weeks
  - Weekly release frequency: episode** older than** 1 month
  - Biweekly release frequency: episode **older than** 2 months
  - Monthly release frequency: episode **older than **4 months
- **IMPORTANT NOTE**: If the result **is not** older than the above thresholds , then the result should be consider recent, therefore, **Excellent rating.**

### Serial type of Podcasts
**Rating:Excellent:**
- Recent episodes, **except trailer episodes**, of the current season
- Recent follow-up/bonus episodes from a past season
- First episode of the season (S1E1 , S2E1, S3E1…)
**Good:**
- All other episodes that are **not** from the current season
- **Not **a recent follow-up/bonus episodes from old “cases”
- Season’s trailer
- **Not** the first episode of the season (S1E2, S3E4, S2E8...)
**Acceptable:**
- Episodes from the secondary intent podcast show
**Unacceptable: Off-Topic:**
- Episodes that are unrelated to the Podcast Show and would not satisfy a secondary intent

## Queries without singular, navigational results
In the Podcasts Search Evaluation, there are multiple query types without a singular navigational result available. These query types include: **Ambiguous, Browse, Category, Podcast Topic/Keyword, Provider, Podcast Host, **and** Person.**  Unlike Navigational queries, users are not searching for a singular specific Podcast, Podcast Episode, or Channel. Instead, multiple pieces of content can satisfy the users’ primary intent and/or secondary intents.

### Rating Scale for Queries without a singular, navigational result
The highest applicable rating for non-navigational query results is **Excellent**.**Excellent**: A piece of content which clearly satisfies a likely primary intent of the query.**Good**: A piece of content which only partially satisfies the intent of the query, but is still high-quality**OR** satisfies the intent of the query but is lower quality**OR **satisfies a secondary intent of the query.**Acceptable**: A piece of content that is related to the query, but may be of poor quality, not popular, or poorly satisfies a secondary intent of the query.**Unacceptable: Off-Topic**: A piece of content that does not satisfy any of the above conditions.**Unacceptable: Misleading **- This rating should be used for content that is likely to intentionally mislead users about its authorship and/or non-podcast content.**Problem: Other**: A problem with the task in BaseLine, for example the result image thumbnail and the result show/episode title are missing, making it impossible to rate the result.
