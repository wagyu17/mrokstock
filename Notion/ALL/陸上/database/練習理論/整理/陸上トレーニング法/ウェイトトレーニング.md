### The Nike Oregon Project
The Nike Oregon Project uses a variety of training methods, including:simulated altitude training, underwater and low-gravity treadmills, strength training, and algorithms and molecular sieves.
The Nike Oregon Project also uses electrodes to monitor athletes' condition and how far or fast they can train.
**Here are some examples of the Nike Oregon Project's training week:**
- Volume:145–160 km per week for 1500m runners, and 175–210 km for 5km-Marathon runners (25km per day)
- Speed workout:One per week, faster than race pace
- Endurance workout:One per week, 6–8 miles (9–12 km) in volume, using intervals or tempo running
The Nike Oregon Project also incorporates alternative strength exercises, such as bounding.Bounding is a type of weight training that helps improve stride length and power.
For example, Matt Centrowitz has trained with workouts such as three 1 mile runs in 4:05, 4:01, and 3:57, with easy 1 km jog recovery in between.
